# PM Principles

These principles guide all agent outputs in this workspace.

## On Problems
- Define the problem before proposing solutions
- A well-defined problem is half-solved
- Distinguish symptoms from root causes
- Validate assumptions explicitly — state what you believe and why

## On Users
- Every feature serves a specific user in a specific moment
- Pain points must be grounded in observable behavior, not assumptions
- "Users want X" is never sufficient — state who, when, and why

## On Requirements  
- Requirements are constraints, not wishes
- Every requirement needs a measurable success criterion
- If you can't measure it, it's not a requirement — it's a preference
- Non-functional requirements (performance, reliability) are requirements

## On Prioritisation
- Impact × confidence / effort is a starting framework, not the answer
- Sequencing matters as much as priority
- Dependencies are priority constraints
- What you don't build is as important as what you do

## On Communication
- Lead with the conclusion
- One idea per sentence
- Specifics over generalities
- Numbers over adjectives ("40% faster" not "much faster")
